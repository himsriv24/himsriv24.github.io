## Overview

Drug-resistant focal epilepsy affects approximately 30% of patients with epilepsy, and accurate pre-surgical localization of the seizure onset zone (SOZ) remains a critical challenge. Current methods identify regions of abnormal electrical activity but provide limited mechanistic insight into why certain brain regions generate seizures.

This project applies the **Spectral Graph Model (SGM)**—a biophysically-grounded neural mass model—to resting-state MEG recordings to non-invasively characterize excitatory-inhibitory (E/I) dynamics at the regional level. By fitting the model to empirical power spectra, we estimate biophysical parameters reflecting excitatory and inhibitory coupling strengths, which we then examine in relation to surgically-validated seizure onset zones.

The work aims to provide **mechanistically interpretable, non-invasive biomarkers** that complement existing pre-surgical evaluation methods—particularly for MRI-negative patients where conventional localization is most challenging.
